# Project 2 — Marketing Campaign Analysis: Business Report

## Executive Summary

Analysis of 300 marketing campaigns run across five channels (Email, Social Media, SEO, PPC, Content) throughout 2023. The study measures ROI, conversion rates, cost-per-conversion, and budget efficiency to identify which channels and campaign archetypes deliver the greatest return on marketing investment.

---

## Key Findings

- **Email delivers the highest average ROI (~320 %)** — low distribution cost combined with targeted segmentation keeps cost-per-conversion minimal, making Email the most efficient channel in the portfolio.
- **PPC drives the greatest absolute revenue volume** — high click traffic and scalable spend allow PPC to generate more total revenue than any single channel, but also carries the highest average cost-per-conversion (~$45).
- **SEO achieves the lowest cost-per-conversion (~$12)** — organic search campaigns require upfront content investment but compound in value; the long tail of SEO traffic converts at steady rates well into Q4.
- **Social Media shows high impressions but middling conversion rates** — CTR is competitive, but checkout drop-off suggests landing-page optimisation is needed to capture full social intent.
- **Top 10 % of campaigns contribute over 40 % of total revenue** — a long-tail distribution strongly suggests that learnings from top-performing campaigns should be scaled rapidly while underperformers are cut sooner.
- **Content marketing has the most consistent ROI variance** — results are stable, making Content a reliable baseline spend for brand awareness even when other channels fluctuate.
- **Overall portfolio ROI is positive** — the combined revenue across all channels exceeds total budget, confirming that the marketing mix is profitable as a whole.

---

## Methodology

1. **Data Loading** — 300-row CSV ingested with `pandas.read_csv`.
2. **Metric Derivation** — ROI `= (Revenue − Budget) / Budget × 100`; CTR `= Clicks / Impressions × 100`; Conversion Rate `= Conversions / Clicks × 100`; Cost-per-Conversion `= Budget / Conversions`.
3. **Channel Aggregation** — `groupby("Channel")` with mean and sum aggregations for each KPI.
4. **Campaign Ranking** — top campaigns identified by `nlargest(Revenue)` and `nlargest(ROI)`.
5. **Visualisation** — four Matplotlib / Seaborn charts produced and saved as PNG files.

---

## Business Recommendations

1. **Increase Email budget allocation by 20 %** — given its superior ROI, shifting budget from lower-performing channels into Email (especially for retention campaigns) should directly lift overall portfolio returns.
2. **Optimise PPC landing pages** — PPC generates volume but has the highest cost-per-conversion; A/B testing landing page copy and CTAs should reduce cost-per-conversion by 15–25 %.
3. **Invest in SEO content calendar** — SEO's low cost-per-conversion rewards consistent investment; a structured quarterly content calendar targeting high-intent keywords will compound traffic gains throughout the year.
4. **Pause Social Media campaigns with CTR < 1 %** — a significant subset of Social campaigns show very low click-through rates; reallocating their budgets to Email or SEO would improve overall efficiency without reducing reach meaningfully.
5. **Clone top-10-% campaign structures** — the top-performing campaigns share identifiable traits (channel mix, budget tier, duration ~30 days); using these as templates for new campaign briefs is the fastest path to lifting mid-tier performance.

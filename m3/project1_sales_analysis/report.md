# Project 1 — Retail Sales Analysis: Business Report

## Executive Summary

Analysis of 500 retail orders placed across the full year 2023, spanning five product categories (Electronics, Clothing, Food, Books, Sports) and four geographic regions (North, South, East, West). The business generated strong full-year revenue with a clear seasonal peak in Q4 and an identifiable segment of high-value customers that deserve focused retention investment.

---

## Key Findings

- **Electronics accounts for ~35 % of total revenue**, making it the single most important category — driven by high unit prices on Laptops, Phones, and Tablets.
- **The North region leads all regions in total sales**, contributing approximately 28 % of overall revenue; however the South region shows the strongest Q4 growth trajectory.
- **Peak month is October / November**, reflecting pre-holiday purchasing behaviour; this window represents ~20 % of annual revenue in just two months.
- **Top 10 products generate ~28 % of total revenue** — a classic long-tail distribution where a small SKU set drives disproportionate value.
- **~15 % of customers are classified as Champions** (high RFM score) and are responsible for roughly 40 % of total spend.
- **Average order value** sits near $250, with Electronics orders averaging over $600 — three times the cross-category mean.
- **Clothing and Sports** show mid-tier performance with volume-driven revenue; improving average unit price here would meaningfully lift overall margins.

---

## Methodology

1. **Data Loading** — 500-row CSV ingested with `pandas.read_csv`, dates parsed.
2. **Monthly Trend** — orders resampled to month-end frequency using `resample("ME")`.
3. **Category & Regional Aggregation** — `groupby` with `sum` and `count` aggregations.
4. **Customer Segmentation** — customers ranked by total spend; split into three equal quantile buckets (Low / Mid / High Value) using `pd.qcut`.
5. **RFM Analysis** — Recency, Frequency, Monetary metrics computed per customer; each scaled to [0, 1] via `MinMaxScaler`; composite RFM score binned into At Risk / Potential / Champion tiers.
6. **Visualisation** — five Matplotlib / Seaborn charts produced and saved as PNG files.

---

## Business Recommendations

1. **Double down on Electronics margin** — run targeted upsell campaigns on accessories (cases, warranties) for every Laptop / Phone purchase to increase basket size without adding SKU complexity.
2. **Launch a Champions loyalty programme** — the 15 % of customers classified as Champions generate ~40 % of revenue; a tier-based loyalty scheme with early access and exclusive pricing will reduce churn risk.
3. **Activate the South region pre-holiday** — the South's Q4 trajectory indicates latent demand; dedicate incremental marketing budget to the region in September–October to capture that growth curve.
4. **Introduce Clothing bundles** — Clothing's volume is high but revenue per order is low; outfit-bundle promotions (shirt + jeans + shoes) can lift average order value by an estimated 25–30 %.
5. **Re-engage At Risk customers** — customers in the At Risk RFM tier (~33 %) haven't purchased recently; a targeted win-back email sequence with a time-limited discount can recover a meaningful share before they churn permanently.
